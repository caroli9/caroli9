250 Million Kids Can't Read This

    LET'S CHANGE THAT

Literacy is a problem in America,
and you can help.

You don't have to be a millionaire to make an impact. Everyone deserves a quality education and you can help provide it. The fight for literacy begins one volunteer, one classroom, and one student at a time.

Give your time

Teach for America helps poverty-stricken school districts across the United States. Learn more about the organization and how joining the TFA Corps of teachers can make a difference.

Give your Skills

Reading out loud to children is one of the most effective ways to stimulate cognitive skills, while building motivation, memory and curiosity. Volunteer with a local school or an after school program to directly impact literacy rates in your neighboorhood.

Make the Fight your Career

You don't have to become a teacher to impact literacy. There's a whole new industry in the tech world centered around improving educaiton. Want to find out more? Read my blog post, Join the Movement. Save Education.

Shop for a Cause

When money and time are tight, you can find other ways to help out literacy and education incentives. Need new office supples? Buy them from an orginazation that matches your purchase to help a school in need. Make your purchases matter. For a list of companies that use your purchase to help education and literacy, read my blog post Confessions of a Philanthropist.

Teach For America

Teach for America recruit's remarkable and diverse individuals to become teachers in low-income communities. They commit to teach for two years and are hired by our partner public schools across the country. During these two years they are called corps members.

About this Website

Over the course of a semester for a Writing in Digital Media class, a group of four Virginia Tech students worked to advocate for public education with a focus on Teach For America. This website is a result of that work. We collaborated on our video and podcast, but the blogpost were individual works. If you'd like to take a look at what the other three group members wrote in their blogs, you can visit their sites here, here, and here.
